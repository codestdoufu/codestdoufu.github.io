About Me

Builder, coder,
problem-solver.

I'm David Fu — a Computer Engineering student at NYU Tandon (class of 2029), based out of Brooklyn, NY. I grew up tinkering: taking apart computers, building custom PCs for people in the neighborhood, and teaching myself to code through competitive programming and game development.

I reached USACO Platinum — one of the top tiers in competitive programming — and qualified for the AIME in both 2023 and 2024. But beyond competitions, I care about making things that actually work and help people. That's what drives everything I build.

Outside of code I've logged over 600 volunteer hours — teaching classes on Zoom, distributing food, organizing shelter donations, and baking cookies for local communities. I think being a good engineer means caring about the people your work touches.

I'm fluent in English and Mandarin, comfortable on Linux, and always looking for the next thing to build. If you want to work together or just talk tech — reach out.
